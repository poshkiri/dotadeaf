# InterestingDeaf - Product Scope

## Product Vision
InterestingDeaf is a focused platform that helps deaf and hard-of-hearing Dota 2 players find teammates, present their play style, communicate directly, and stay informed about game updates in Russian.

The product vision for MVP is practical and narrow: enable core player discovery and communication needs with minimal complexity.

## Target Audience
- Deaf and hard-of-hearing Dota 2 players.
- Russian-speaking users who need accessible Dota 2 patch content in Russian.
- Players seeking teammates with compatible roles, skill levels, and communication preferences.

## MVP Goals
- Provide reliable account access and profile identity.
- Enable users to create clear player profiles and discover relevant players quickly.
- Support direct one-to-one communication through basic personal chat.
- Deliver Russian-language Dota 2 patch pages as a core informational feature.
- Show simple online/activity status to improve communication timing.

## MVP Feature List
The MVP scope includes only the following features:
- Authentication.
- Profile creation.
- Player profiles.
- Player search with filters.
- Basic personal chat.
- Dota 2 patch pages in Russian.
- Simple online/activity status.

## Non-Goals (Explicitly Out of Scope for MVP)
The MVP must not include:
- Support for multiple games.
- Video chat.
- Tournaments.
- Advanced statistics integrations.
- Admin panel (unless explicitly requested later).
- AI features.
- Mobile app.
- Complex social mechanics.

## Scope Control Rules
1. Product decisions must remain within the MVP feature list.
2. Any item listed in Non-Goals must not be implemented without explicit scope approval.
3. No hidden scope growth is allowed through optional add-ons, "quick extras," or speculative enhancements.
4. New feature requests must be evaluated as post-MVP unless formally approved for inclusion.
5. When uncertainty exists, the default decision is to keep the scope unchanged and request clarification.

## Single-Game Rule for MVP
For MVP, Dota 2 is the only supported game.  
No multi-game abstractions, game-agnostic product logic, or additional game support may be introduced unless explicitly approved after MVP.
